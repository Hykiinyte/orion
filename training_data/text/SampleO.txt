Chaos and order are fundamental aspects that are ever present in the universe.

People have found a way to measure the "amount" of order in the universe, or just in something in particular. That measurement is called "entropy". Entropy is how orderly or chaotic a given mixture is. Take the universe as an example, we currently live in a state of high entropy. Gas and material are strewn all across the universe in unequally. Gas collapses into stars while dust forms into planet, things are always changing- be it in a few years or a billion years. At the beginning of the universe before the big bang and at the heat death of the universe, it will be low entropy- which is total order.

Entropy may appear in anything- like mixtures, a mixture can have high entropy when you mix materials, but once you have even distributed all of the material, is becomes low entropy. Entropy can also be used mathematically, such as in AI. AI uses cross entropy loss to find order, particularly in Large Language Models or LLMs while in the training process in order to determine the proper order of words and correct usage of language. From the initial start being essentially unintelligible or unreadable, an AI in training will read its previous inferences and compare it to the training data, and determine entropy loss due to corrections in language training in its next epoch.

Entropy is generally a very misunderstood concept by most people. Many misunderstandings on what entropy is, surrounds the idea of chaos and order- some people may think it is a quantification of chaos, but it is more so the quantification of a materials in a mixture- or items in a collection in the case of AI- order and distribution around it. High entropy means less order, while low entropy means more order.
